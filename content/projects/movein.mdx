## What I built

MoveIn reads a neighbourhood from official open data and lays it out like a
surveyor's trade-off receipt — five indicators, each beside the figure it came
from. It shows the evidence; it never tells you a place is "good" or "safe".

_MoveIn is the web app; `uk-housing-decision-support` is the repository. The
"where to live" framing is the vehicle — the substance is the pipeline, the
dimensional and decision modelling, the tests, and the explainability layer._

## The data engine

Seven official open-data sources, rolled to MSOA grain across all 7,264
England & Wales neighbourhoods:

- **Sale-market context** — 4.99M Land Registry transactions (2021–2025)
- **Geography** — ONSPD bridge, 2.73M postcodes (99.999% coverage)
- **Rent & affordability** — ONS PIPR, including per-bedroom rents
- **Energy** — 23.5M EPC certificates → per-area median band
- **Crime** — 17.1M police street crimes as a rate (an indicator, never a verdict)
- **Flood & planning** — spatial point-in-polygon; flood band + constraint count
- **Convenience** — 437k OpenStreetMap amenities, nearest + walkable count

Sources → staging → intermediate → marts, tested at every layer: **197 dbt
data tests + 2 dbt unit tests**, with lineage and column-level docs published
to GitHub Pages on every push. Each source is fixture-default for fast,
reproducible CI with a real-data toggle for production builds.

## Explainable scoring — readable top to bottom

The score is one SQL transformation (`rpt_neighbourhood_score`), not a black
box: median-anchored, winsorised normalisation to 0–100 (so magnitude
survives, unlike a pure percentile rank); an overall score as a weighted
_geometric_ mean so one excellent pillar can't mask a poor one; missing
indicators dropped — never zeroed — lowering the area's confidence level
instead of silently penalising it; and every score shipped beside its raw
figure with a generated "why this area" sentence.

## Orchestration and delivery

Land Registry data refreshes monthly, so the refresh is a **Dagster asset
graph**: ingestion (with a pre-dbt data-quality gate) → dbt → a slim
`decision.duckdb` extract. A thin **FastAPI service** (Fly.io) serves that
extract publicly with OpenAPI docs, and the **Next.js site** (Vercel) — search,
compare, listing checker, ~7k programmatic area/town/region/rent pages — is
just one HTTP client of it. Scoring logic lives in exactly one place.

## Honest limits

A reference analytics-engineering project, not a product — the UK area-data
space is already well served, and the repo says so up front. Indicators, never
verdicts. Door-to-door commute time is the one remaining planned signal
(station proximity is covered), and the data refreshes on a monthly cadence,
not in real time.
